The Science

The Science Behind Light-Infused Water

The story of light-infused water begins with one of science's most important questions: What is light?

For centuries, some of the world's greatest scientific minds have helped shape our understanding.

1672
Isaac Newton
Isaac Newton described light as streams of tiny particles traveling in straight lines, supporting the idea that light behaves like particles.
1678
Christian Huygens
Christian Huygens proposed that light travels as waves, helping explain phenomena such as reflection and refraction.
1801
Thomas Young
Thomas Young's famous double-slit experiment demonstrated that light behaves like a wave, producing interference patterns through constructive and destructive interference.
1860s
James Clerk Maxwell
James Clerk Maxwell unified electricity and magnetism through Maxwell's equations, revealing that light is an electromagnetic wave and part of the electromagnetic spectrum.
1905
Albert Einstein
Albert Einstein showed that light can also behave as discrete particles, later called photons, helping explain the photoelectric effect.
The Connection

From Light to Water

By the 1940s, these ideas were brought together through Quantum Electrodynamics, or QED, developed through the work of scientists including Richard Feynman, Julian Schwinger, Sin-Itiro Tomonaga, and Freeman Dyson.
